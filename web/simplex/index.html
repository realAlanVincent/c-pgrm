Simplex Method Solution

## Problem Statement

Problem Statement

Maximize \( Z = 3x_1 + 2x_2 + 4x_3 \) subject to the constraints:

  • \( 2x_1 + 3x_2 \leq 8 \)
  • \( 2x_2 + 5x_3 \leq 10 \)
  • \( x_1, x_2, x_3 \geq 0 \)

We will solve this linear programming problem using the simplex method, introducing slack variables \( S_1 \) and \( S_2 \) to convert the inequalities into equalities.

## Initial Tableau

Initial Tableau

We start with an initial basic feasible solution where the slack variables \( S_1 \) and \( S_2 \) are basic, and \( x_1, x_2, x_3 \) are non-basic (set to zero). Thus:

  • \( S_1 = 8 \)
  • \( S_2 = 10 \)
  • \( Z = 0 \)

The \( c_j \) values are the coefficients from the objective function: 3 for \( x_1 \), 2 for \( x_2 \), 4 for \( x_3 \), and 0 for \( S_1 \) and \( S_2 \). The initial tableau is:

\( c_j \) \( B \) \( b \) \( x_1 \) \( x_2 \) \( x_3 \) \( S_1 \) \( S_2 \) Min Ratio
0 \( S_1 \) 8 2 3 0 1 0
0 \( S_2 \) 10 0 2 5 0 1
\( Z \) 0 0 0 0 0 0
\( c_j - z_j \) 3 2 4 0 0

The positive values in the \( c_j - z_j \) row (3, 2, 4) indicate that the solution is not optimal. We select the entering variable with the largest positive value: \( x_3 \) with \( c_j - z_j = 4 \).

## First Iteration – Pivot on \( x_3 \)

First Iteration – Pivot on \( x_3 \)

Steps:

  • Entering Variable: \( x_3 \) (since \( c_j - z_j = 4 \) is the largest positive value).
  • Pivot Column: \( x_3 \) column coefficients are 0 and 5.
  • Min Ratio Test: For rows where the pivot column coefficient > 0:
    • \( S_1 \): \( 8 / 0 \), not applicable (coefficient is 0).
    • \( S_2 \): \( 10 / 5 = 2 \).
    Minimum ratio is 2, so \( S_2 \) leaves.
  • Leaving Variable: \( S_2 \).
  • Pivot Element: 5 (intersection of \( S_2 \) row and \( x_3 \) column).

Row Operations:

  1. New \( x_3 \) Row: \( S_2 \) row ÷ 5 = [0, \( \frac{2}{5} \), 1, 0, \( \frac{1}{5} \), \( \frac{10}{5} = 2 \)].
  2. New \( S_1 \) Row: Unchanged since \( x_3 \) coefficient is 0: [2, 3, 0, 1, 0, 8].
  3. New \( Z \) Row: Update using \( c_B \): \( S_1 = 0 \), \( x_3 = 4 \).
    • For each column, \( z_j = 0 \cdot a_{1j} + 4 \cdot a_{2j} \):
    • \( x_1 \): \( 0 \cdot 2 + 4 \cdot 0 = 0 \)
    • \( x_2 \): \( 0 \cdot 3 + 4 \cdot \frac{2}{5} = \frac{8}{5} \)
    • \( x_3 \): \( 0 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot 1 = 4 \)
    • \( S_1 \): \( 0 \cdot 1 + 4 \cdot 0 = 0 \)
    • \( S_2 \): \( 0 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot \frac{1}{5} = \frac{4}{5} \)
    • \( b \): \( 0 \cdot 8 + 4 \cdot 2 = 8 \)
    • \( c_j - z_j \): Subtract from \( c_j \):
      • \( x_1 \): \( 3 - 0 = 3 \)
      • \( x_2 \): \( 2 - \frac{8}{5} = \frac{10}{5} - \frac{8}{5} = \frac{2}{5} \)
      • \( x_3 \): \( 4 - 4 = 0 \)
      • \( S_1 \): \( 0 - 0 = 0 \)
      • \( S_2 \): \( 0 - \frac{4}{5} = -\frac{4}{5} \)

The updated tableau is:

\( c_j \) \( B \) \( b \) \( x_1 \) \( x_2 \) \( x_3 \) \( S_1 \) \( S_2 \) Min Ratio
0 \( S_1 \) 8 2 3 0 1 0
4 \( x_3 \) 2 0 \( \frac{2}{5} \) 1 0 \( \frac{1}{5} \)
\( Z \) 8 0 \( \frac{8}{5} \) 4 0 \( \frac{4}{5} \)
\( c_j - z_j \) 3 \( \frac{2}{5} \) 0 0 \( -\frac{4}{5} \)

Since there are still positive values in the \( c_j - z_j \) row (e.g., 3 and \( \frac{2}{5} \)), the solution is not yet optimal. We proceed to the next iteration.

## Second Iteration – Pivot on \( x_1 \)

Second Iteration – Pivot on \( x_1 \)

Steps:

  • Entering Variable: \( x_1 \) (since \( c_j - z_j = 3 \) is the largest positive value).
  • Pivot Column: \( x_1 \) column coefficients are 2 and 0.
  • Min Ratio Test: For rows where the pivot column coefficient > 0:
    • \( S_1 \): \( 8 / 2 = 4 \)
    • \( x_3 \): \( 2 / 0 \), not applicable.
    Minimum ratio is 4, so \( S_1 \) leaves.
  • Leaving Variable: \( S_1 \).
  • Pivot Element: 2 (intersection of \( S_1 \) row and \( x_1 \) column).

Row Operations:

  1. New \( x_1 \) Row: \( S_1 \) row ÷ 2 = [1, \( \frac{3}{2} \), 0, \( \frac{1}{2} \), 0, \( \frac{8}{2} = 4 \)].
  2. New \( x_3 \) Row: Unchanged since \( x_1 \) coefficient is 0: [0, \( \frac{2}{5} \), 1, 0, \( \frac{1}{5} \), 2].
  3. New \( Z \) Row: Update using \( c_B \): \( x_1 = 3 \), \( x_3 = 4 \).
    • For each column, \( z_j = 3 \cdot a_{1j} + 4 \cdot a_{2j} \):
    • \( x_1 \): \( 3 \cdot 1 + 4 \cdot 0 = 3 \)
    • \( x_2 \): \( 3 \cdot \frac{3}{2} + 4 \cdot \frac{2}{5} = \frac{9}{2} + \frac{8}{5} = \frac{45}{10} + \frac{16}{10} = \frac{61}{10} \)
    • \( x_3 \): \( 3 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot 1 = 4 \)
    • \( S_1 \): \( 3 \cdot \frac{1}{2} + 4 \cdot 0 = \frac{3}{2} \)
    • \( S_2 \): \( 3 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot \frac{1}{5} = \frac{4}{5} \)
    • \( b \): \( 3 \cdot 4 + 4 \cdot 2 = 12 + 8 = 20 \)
    • \( c_j - z_j \):
      • \( x_1 \): \( 3 - 3 = 0 \)
      • \( x_2 \): \( 2 - \frac{61}{10} = \frac{20}{10} - \frac{61}{10} = -\frac{41}{10} \)
      • \( x_3 \): \( 4 - 4 = 0 \)
      • \( S_1 \): \( 0 - \frac{3}{2} = -\frac{3}{2} \)
      • \( S_2 \): \( 0 - \frac{4}{5} = -\frac{4}{5} \)

The updated tableau is:

\( c_j \) \( B \) \( b \) \( x_1 \) \( x_2 \) \( x_3 \) \( S_1 \) \( S_2 \) Min Ratio
3 \( x_1 \) 4 1 \( \frac{3}{2} \) 0 \( \frac{1}{2} \) 0
4 \( x_3 \) 2 0 \( \frac{2}{5} \) 1 0 \( \frac{1}{5} \)
\( Z \) 20 3 \( \frac{61}{10} \) 4 \( \frac{3}{2} \) \( \frac{4}{5} \)
\( c_j - z_j \) 0 \( -\frac{41}{10} \) 0 \( -\frac{3}{2} \) \( -\frac{4}{5} \)

All values in the \( c_j - z_j \) row are now non-positive (\( \leq 0 \)), indicating that the solution is optimal.

## Final Solution

Final Solution

Optimal Solution:

From the final tableau: - \( x_1 = 4 \) - \( x_3 = 2 \) - \( x_2 = 0 \) (non-basic) - \( S_1 = 0 \) (non-basic) - \( S_2 = 0 \) (non-basic) - \( Z = 20 \)

The optimal value of the objective function is \( Z = 20 \) at \( x_1 = 4 \), \( x_2 = 0 \), \( x_3 = 2 \).

## Verification

Verification

Substitute \( x_1 = 4 \), \( x_2 = 0 \), \( x_3 = 2 \) into the objective function and constraints:

  • Objective Function: \( Z = 3x_1 + 2x_2 + 4x_3 = 3 \cdot 4 + 2 \cdot 0 + 4 \cdot 2 = 12 + 0 + 8 = 20 \), matches the tableau.
  • Constraint 1: \( 2x_1 + 3x_2 = 2 \cdot 4 + 3 \cdot 0 = 8 \leq 8 \), satisfied.
  • Constraint 2: \( 2x_2 + 5x_3 = 2 \cdot 0 + 5 \cdot 2 = 0 + 10 = 10 \leq 10 \), satisfied.
  • Non-negativity: \( x_1 = 4 \geq 0 \), \( x_2 = 0 \geq 0 \), \( x_3 = 2 \geq 0 \), satisfied.

The solution satisfies all constraints and matches the computed \( Z \), confirming its correctness.
